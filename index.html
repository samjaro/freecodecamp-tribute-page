Timeline of Dr Sack's life

  • 1933 - Born in London
  • 1960 - Received his medical degree from The Queen's College, Oxford
  • 1965 - He relocated to New York, where he worked under a paid fellowship in neurochemistry and neuropathology at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine.
  • 1966 - He began serving as neurologist at Beth Abraham Hospital's chronic-care facility in the Bronx.
  • 1970 - Publishes his first book Migraine
  • 1972 - Publishes his book Awakenings which describes his experiences using the new drug levodopa on Beth Abraham Hospital post-encephalitic patients.
  • 1974 - Has a near fatal accident which inspires him to write his next book, A leg to stand on
  • 1990 - A feature film is made of his book Awakenings, starring Robin Williams
  • 1995 - Publishes 'An Anthropologist on Mars'
  • 1997 - Publishes 'The Island of the colourblind'
  • 2001 - Publishes 'Uncle Tungsten: Memories of a Chemical' Boyhood
  • 2007 - Publishese 'Musicophilia: Tales of Music and the Brain'
  • 2007 - Sacks received the position "Columbia Artist" from Columbia University, a post that was created specifically for him and that gave him unconstrained access to the university
  • 2008 - Sacks was awarded a CBE for services to medicine
  • 2015 - Sacks founded a nonprofit organization established to increase understanding of the brain through using narrative nonfiction and case histories, with goals that include publishing some of Sacks's unpublished writings, and making his vast amount of unpublished writings available for scholarly study.
  • 2015 - dies at the age of 82.
